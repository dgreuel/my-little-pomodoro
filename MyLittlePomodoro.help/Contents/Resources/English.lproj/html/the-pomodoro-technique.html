The Pomodoro Technique and My Little Pomodoro

The Pomodoro Technique is a time management method developed by Francesco Cirillo in the 1980s. It uses a 25 minute work period, called a "pomodoro", followed by breaks.

There are five basic steps to using the technique:

  1. Choose a task to be accomplished, and write it on a piece of paper.
  2. Set the timer to 25 minutes.
  3. Work on the task until the timer rings, then put a mark next to the task.
  4. Take a short break, between 3-5 minutes.
  5. Every 4 Pomodoros, take a long break (15-30 minutes).

My Little Pomodoro aims to be the Pomodoro timer, and the paper and pen used for recording tasks, that you use while working. And just in case the 25/5/15 minute split does not work for you, My Little Pomodoro lets you adjust these timer lengths.
